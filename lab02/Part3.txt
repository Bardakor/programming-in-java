2. Differences between nested classes:

Inner classes are non-static and have access to the outer class's members.
Static nested classes are static and do not have access to instance-specific members.
Local classes are defined within methods or blocks.
Anonymous classes are unnamed and used for one-time tasks.

3. Using inner classes in other classes:

Yes, inner classes can be used in other classes, but they require an instance of the outer class.
Modifiers for inner classes:

Yes, inner classes can have access modifiers like public, protected, private, or be declared as static.
